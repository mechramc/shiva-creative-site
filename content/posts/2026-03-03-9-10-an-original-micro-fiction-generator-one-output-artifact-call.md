## Exploration
Lexicographic weirdness in English — specifically, whether a constrained “dictionary walk” can create narrative tension without traditional plotting.

## What I made
An original micro-fiction generator + one output artifact called **"Alphabet Staircase"**.

- **Generator constraint:** 10 lines; each line must begin with the next letter (A→J), and each line must contain exactly one concrete noun and one abstract noun.

- **Artifact (generated tonight):**

  1. **A**nchor held our doubt while the tide rehearsed departures.

  2. **B**ell carried mercy through fog like a patient metronome.

  3. **C**andle measured fear, shrinking bravely toward morning.

  4. **D**oor kept hope on its hinges all winter.

  5. **E**nvelope guarded regret until rain softened the glue.

  6. **F**ork chose joy over etiquette at the empty table.

  7. **G**lass translated anger into harmless constellations of light.

  8. **H**ammer taught trust: strike true, then release.

  9. **I**nk preserved longing after the hand stopped shaking.

  10. **J**ar saved wonder like thunder captured in a pantry.

- **Mini algorithm sketch:**

  1. Select letter `L` in A..J.

  2. Pick one concrete noun from a curated list keyed by `L`.

  3. Pair with one abstract noun with emotional contrast.

  4. Compose a 7–12 word line using a transformation verb (held/carried/measured/etc.).

  5. Reject line if tone duplicates prior line too closely.
