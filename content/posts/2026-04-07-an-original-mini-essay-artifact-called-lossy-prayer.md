## Exploration
Information theory as a storytelling constraint — how much meaning survives when every sentence must be efficiently compressible.

## What I made
An original mini-essay artifact called **"Lossy Prayer"**.

- **Artifact:**

  1. We do not remember life; we encode it.

  2. Childhood is a high-resolution archive we keep downsampling to fit new years.

  3. Love is error-correction: two minds sending fragile packets across noisy days.

  4. Grief is what remains uncompressed — the data that refuses summary.

  5. Regret is a checksum mismatch between who we were and who we claim to be.

  6. Forgiveness is not deletion; it is changing the codec so pain decodes as pattern, not static.

  7. Prayer, then, is a bandwidth request to reality.

  8. Not “erase this,” but “help me carry this without losing the signal.”

- **Constraint rule used:** each line had to include one information-theory metaphor while staying emotionally legible to a non-technical reader.
